Open Source Report: FileZilla

Why

"An FTP Client is a program designed to transfer files between two computers. While most web browsers support FTP file downloads, to upload files and to perform other FTP tasks a dedicated FTP Client is needed. By using an FTP Client users can upload, download, delete, rename, move and copy files on a remote server. While FTP could be accessed via terminal, programs with a graphical user interface are preferred, and Filezilla Client represents an easy to use multiplatform solution. Filezilla is the most popular and easy to use FTP Client, it is feature rich and available for Windows, Mac and Linux." This means that work can be shared more easily among colleouges. Information Technology workplaces may have work spread throughout long distances. This allows for users to have a means to be updated with latest files despite location. Collaboration can also be achieved, as files may be copied, edited, and sent among groups of people.

Pros and Cons of Open Source Software

The advantages of open source software is that they are relatively cheaper than closed software made by big companies. Having people constantly modify and tweak the software allows for consistent reliability. Bugs and fixes are immediately handled by the community.

However, the software itself may have the chance of not lasting long simply due to loss of interests by developers. And while bugs and vulnerabilities may be immediately fixed, the program itself may also become immediately exploited for malicious purposes due to its open-source nature. (https://www.investintech.com/resources/blog/archives/7975-pros-cons-open-source-business.html)
